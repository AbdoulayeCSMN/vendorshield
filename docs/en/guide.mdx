This guide walks you through VendorShield in the natural order of use.

## 1. Create your account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up">
    On the sign-in page → **Create an account** (email + password).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Onboarding">
    The onboarding assistant (`/onboarding`) points you to the first actions: import
    suppliers and run an assessment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## 2. Add your suppliers

Two options:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Bulk import (recommended)">
    **Suppliers → Imports → Supplier records**. Upload your CSV/Excel; the **column mapping
    is suggested by AI** (accept or adjust), then confirm. See
    [Imports & AI mapping](/en/features/imports).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Manual entry">
    **Suppliers → Add a supplier**: fill in name, country, category, criticality, spend,
    contract…
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
Only the **name** is required. Scores can be imported if you have them, or computed later
via an assessment.
</Tip>

## 3. Assess risk (24 criteria)

**Assessments → New assessment**: score the supplier across the 4 dimensions (financial,
operational, geopolitical, ESG). On completion, **scores propagate automatically** to the
supplier and the **risk level** is computed. See [Assessments](/en/features/assessments).

## 4. Read the dashboard

The **Dashboard** (`/home`) summarizes your portfolio:
- KPIs (supplier count, average risk, open alerts),
- **world map** of suppliers by country,
- trends (average score, alerts per week),
- top risky suppliers.

## 5. Monitor: alerts & scan

- **Alert rules**: set thresholds (e.g. financial score &lt; 40) → automatic alert when a
  supplier crosses the threshold.
- **Scan now** (Alerts page): detects **expiring contracts/documents** and **stale
  assessments**. See [Alerts & monitoring](/en/features/alerts-monitoring).

<Tip>
In production, wire up **alert emails** and the daily **monitoring scan** — see
[Automation](/en/deployment/automation).
</Tip>

## 6. Analyze exposure & diversify

The **Exposure** page shows your **Spend-at-Risk**, **concentration** and **sole-sources**.
The **Multi-sourcing** panel lists critical dependencies and proposes alternatives; the
**AI strategic advice** button generates a diversification plan. See
[Exposure & multi-sourcing](/en/features/exposure-sourcing).

## 7. Anticipate with ML

On a supplier with **delivery history**, the **Operational predictions** panel estimates the
probability of **delay** and **defects (PPM)**. Click **Recompute** to refresh. A new account
benefits from a **cold-start** (global model) on day one. See
[Predictions & ML](/en/features/predictions-ml).

## 8. Ask the copilot

The **Aboki** copilot (widget or dedicated page) answers questions about your suppliers and
guides you through the app, using **your real data**. Examples:
- "What are my 3 riskiest suppliers?"
- "How do I reduce my dependency in the components category?"

See [Copilot Aboki](/en/features/copilot).

## 9. Compliance & documents

On each supplier record: **Documents & compliance** (certifications, CSRD deadlines),
**Questionnaires** (sent via the supplier portal), **Audits** and **corrective action plans
(CAPA)**.
