The **Exposure** page (`/home/exposure`) gives a portfolio-level view of risk.

## Exposure metrics

| Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Spend-at-Risk** | Σ (spend × incident probability). Share of spend actually exposed. |
| **Concentration (HHI)** | Herfindahl index on spend shares (low / moderate / high). |
| **Top-3 dependency** | Share of spend on the 3 largest suppliers (stress-test). |
| **Sole-sources** | Count and spend of single-source suppliers. |

### How it is computed

**Spend-at-Risk** — spend weighted by each supplier's incident probability:

$$
\text{SaR} = \sum_{k} \text{spend}_k \cdot \left(1 - \frac{S_k}{100}\right)
$$

where $S_k$ is supplier $k$'s global score (0–100); the factor $\left(1 - S_k/100\right)$ is its incident probability.

**Concentration (HHI)** — Herfindahl index on spend shares:

$$
\text{HHI} = \sum_{k} s_k^{2}
\qquad
s_k = \frac{\text{spend}_k}{\text{total spend}} \times 100
$$

$\text{HHI} \le 10\,000$. Guides: $>2500$ **high** concentration, $>1500$ **moderate**, otherwise **low**.

## Multi-sourcing & diversification

The engine flags **dependency risks** and proposes a concrete action:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Critical sole-source" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Critical supplier with no alternative → **qualify a second source**.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Alternatives exist" icon="code-branch">
    Several suppliers in the category → **split the volume**.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

For each case, VendorShield counts the **qualified alternatives** in the same category
(sorted by lowest risk) and shows them as links.

### AI strategic advice

The **"AI strategic advice"** button turns these facts into a **prioritized diversification
plan** (markdown), via Groq → OpenRouter.

<Note>
The advice invents nothing: it cites only your real suppliers and figures. Without an LLM
key, the factual recommendations remain displayed.
</Note>
